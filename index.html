The Chronicles of the Dev Amulet

Chapter I: The Inception

Long before the whispers began…
before reason fractured…
before ambition learned to hunger—

the Realm endured The Inferno.

It was the greatest catastrophe since the ancient Firestorm. Entire kingdoms beneath the authority of High Sovereign Sev were reduced to ash and bone. Smoke choked the heavens for weeks. Settlements vanished. Granaries collapsed. Livestock suffocated in blackened fields.

Thousands died defending land already doomed. Tens of thousands perished in flame. Orphans wandered the roads in silent clusters, clutching memories of homes that no longer existed.

High Sovereign Sev ruled not a single kingdom, but a Realm—its many kings sworn beneath his crown. And now those kings brought him famine.

Though evacuations spared many lives, wealth and harvest were not so fortunate. Silos burned. Fields turned to soot. The royal reserves, though vast, would not last indefinitely.

The people mourned.

Then they demanded.

They demanded action.
They demanded relief.
They demanded that their Sovereign justify his dominion.

Sev understood the arithmetic of starvation. Even if he emptied every silo across the Realm, the grain would not endure the year. Too many farmers had died. Too many hands were buried beneath ash.

He could not seek aid from rival realms beyond his borders. To reveal weakness was to invite invasion.

And so he turned to the one name rulers spoke only in private.

Ryder.

The witch of the eastern marsh.

The Realm had wronged her once—how, few remembered—but resentment lingered in her gaze like oil upon still water.

Sev did not trust her.

He required her.

Together, beneath oath-bound secrecy, they forged the first Amulets of Labor.

The enchantments were precise—crafted to strengthen muscle, extend endurance, sharpen focus. The magic pressed against obsession but stopped short of consuming the soul.

The amulets were distributed among surviving laborers. Fields were ploughed in days that once required weeks. Roads cleared. Walls rose. Rebuilding began at a fevered pace.

Hope returned.

Too soon.

The spring rains never came.

April passed dry.
May followed without storm.
The soil split open like old bone.

The crops withered beneath a merciless sky. Wells thinned. The near-restored harvest began to die.

Famine stood once more at the gates of the Realm.

Sev returned to Ryder.

“There is a magic,” she told him, her voice low as marsh water, “older than crowns and older than famine. It can bend reality. It can command sky and stone. It can will desire into existence.”

“Then we will use it,” Sev replied.

Ryder did not move.

“It does not serve,” she said.
“It devours.”

This magic would grant the bearer their greatest wish. But it would hollow the mind. Strip reason. Magnify ambition until morality splintered. No will was immune. No heart immune.

Sev did not hesitate.

“If my mind must fracture so my people may live, then let it fracture.”

Ryder summoned others in secrecy.

Nicholas, Grand Mage of the Bone Kingdom—one of the kingdoms beneath Sev’s Realm.

Alice of the Emberwilds, whose loyalty bent only toward balance.

And John the Ronin—the most disciplined sorcerer in the known lands.

For three nights they labored.

The marsh recoiled.
The air stilled.
Even flame burned strangely.

From one remaining Amulet of Labor, they re-forged something darker.

They named it The Devourer’s Amulet.

Among themselves, for brevity—and perhaps unease—they called it The Dev Amulet.

Unlike the earlier amulets, which nudged their wearers toward tireless work, this artifact would not whisper.

It would seize.
It would grant.
It would feed.

John, cautious and exacting, wove a failsafe into the enchantment—a reversion spell. Once the wielder’s command was fulfilled, the Dev Amulet would collapse back into an ordinary Amulet of Labor. The corruption would cease.

When the ritual concluded, the four stepped away.

Ryder lingered longest beside the amulet.

Then she withdrew without a word.

Sev took the artifact into his hands.

“I will rain,” he commanded.

The sky obeyed.

Clouds rolled across barren horizons. Thunder split the heavens. Rain fell—heavy, relentless, glorious rain. The fields drank. The crops revived. Wells filled once more.

The Dev Amulet found the task effortless.

But Sev felt it.

Power surged through him like a second pulse.

Why stop at rain?
Why not flood rival realms beyond his dominion?
Why not command gold from stone?
Why not rule without challenge?

The amulet did not speak in words.

It clarified desire.

For a fragile moment, Sev nearly listened.

Then John’s failsafe ignited.

The amulet dimmed. Its pulse subsided. The storm in Sev’s thoughts quieted. The artifact reverted—its surface dull, its presence ordinary.

The Sovereign was whole.

The sorcerers departed.

Nicholas returned to his bone halls.
Alice to the Emberwilds.
John the Ronin vanished months later under circumstances unknown, never realizing what his spell had truly contained.

The harvest was restored. The people praised Sev as savior. And rightly so.

Believing the artifact harmless—and in need of immediate funds to rebuild roads, granaries, and defenses—Sev quietly sold the amulet as one among many tools of labor.

He did not boast of its true power. Dark magic was not something a wise Sovereign advertised.

The artifact passed from vault to merchant stall.

It waited.
It remembered rain.
It remembered command.
It remembered ambition.

The buyer was a cheerful traveling young man—an explorer with bright eyes and steady hands. Skilled with blades and shurikens. Curious. Optimistic. Kind.

He loved the Realm and believed it could be better.

His name was Ducky.
